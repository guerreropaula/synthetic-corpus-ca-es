**Paula Guerrero Castelló**

This respository contains the paper *“Building a Synthetic Parallel Catalan–Spanish Corpus of Literary Texts”*.

## Abstract

This paper describes the design and implementation of a parallel corpus of early
twentieth-century Catalan literary texts and their automatic Spanish translations. Starting
from XML-embedded plain-text documents sourced from the Corpus Textual Informatitzat
de la Llengua Catalana (CTILC), we built a six-script pipeline that (1) loads and normalizes
the raw texts, (2) translates them from Catalan into Spanish using Google’s Gemma-27B-IT,
(3) preprocesses and builds a paragraph-level bilingual corpus, (4) analyzes the distribution
of the resulting corpus, (5) performs a comparative linguistic analysis covering keyword
extraction, n-gram frequencies, and lexical richness metrics, and (6) filters the metadata to
the translated documents. The resulting corpus1 contains 63 documents (1,577,899 Catalan
words; 1,669,666 Spanish words; 30,031 aligned paragraph pairs) published between 1905
and 1948 and spans five dialectal variants of Catalan. The comparative analysis reveals
systematic lexical divergences between the source language and its translations, offering a
quantitative window onto the linguistic distance between Catalan and Spanish as well as the
translation behavior of a large language model on literary prose.
